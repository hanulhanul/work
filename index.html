{ CREATIVE TECHNOLOGY }

I research, ideate, experiment, prototype, iterate, and produce digital artifacts & interactive experiences.

Hi, I'm Hanul Park. My name means "sky" in Korean and is a pure Hangul word. I was born in the 90s and grew up by the ocean & mountains of South Korea. I've been studying, working, and living in New York City since 2018. I completed my BFA in Design & Technology at Parsons School of Design with a minor in Data Visualization and my BA in Culture & Media at Eugene Lang College of Liberal Arts at The New School. Welcome to my site!

My work varies from AI-trained design abstracts to data-driven infographics to mixed reality narratives. The software tools and digital interfaces that I use vary as much as the topics and themes of my work, and I continue to learn new technologies. My diverse body of work shares an overarching drive for subversion of structures and elevation of contents. I believe that these two themes present themselves most brilliantly when explored together.

The thinker and leader of our time Ruha Benjamin said: "Remember to imagine and craft the world you cannot live without, just as you dismantle the ones you cannot live within." My creative practice involves re-interpreting the status quo from critical perspectives, re-imagining its content and structure as thoroughly as possible, and re-constructing them in virtual, physical, and mixed environments. I hope that the process of crafting various versions of the world helps us get close to realizing a better world in our tangible reality.
Born in the mid 90s and raised in South Korea, I got to witness the emergence of digital culture that soon permeated into many elements of contemporary life. Growing up, I communicated with my friends on Korea's very first social network platform Cyworld and read Webtoon, which is digital comics that is produced, distributed, and appreciated entirely online. As an enthusiastic participant of both, I naturally grew an intimate understanding of innovative platforms that can create entirely new elements within our culture. I'm fascinated by how digital culture can change our lives so intricately.

As a creative technologist, I seek imaginative ways to use information technology. As an experimental programmer, I utilize engineering tools to draw fun computational forms. As an interdisciplinary designer, I produce interactive experiences using various software tools. As a multimedia artist, I capture and critique the visual culture of today and the possible future through mixed media. Above all, I am always curious, and such aspect of myself has led me to work with what I do today. The intersection of art, media, and technology excites me profoundly.
